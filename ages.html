CAMP/pixel offers programs for our youngest learners, ages 7-9. We align with early elementary-level academic and reading levels to create a visual, hands-on learning experience for kids ready to imagine with us.

Each of our topics is available in fun, intriguing, inquiry-based activities. Every camper will spend his or her learning time asking questions, imagining and building projects, and being assisted by our expert instructors. Built into the camp day are plenty of screen breaks, physical activities, food breaks, and opportunity to learn and play with one another.

CAMP/pixel is building an elite team of motivated high-school students ages 15-17. Participating teens will develop fluency in 21st-century technology, build future portfolio and application materials, learn crucial early job skills, and work first-hand with kids and like-minded teens. Our goal is to help teens set goals both in their own work and with others, and to help them see those ideas become realities.

Counselor In Training
(C.I.T.) Position
Teens ages 15 - 17
4-week project-based summer program for tech-loving teens
No cost to apply or participate
Hands-on experience with cool, cutting-edge technology
Experience and training that will help you in school, college, and beyond

The CAMP/pixel C.I.T. position is a perfect blend of LEARNING and LEADING for teens with a passion for technology and education. Our C.I.T.'s spend some of each day learning new skills and working with our expert instructors to program, code, produce, design, and invent new products. The rest of the day is dedicated to working with campers in a leadership role to help them learn a new skill, complete a camp challenge, or just have fun.

Learning
  • Cutting-edge and professional grade technology.
  • Mentoring and skills tutoring from our expert instructors.
  • Leadership training and teamwork experience.
  • Guidance towards personal goal-setting and self-self-efficacy.
Leading
  • Hands-on skill development, helping kids learn technology.
  • Opportunities to lead activities and screen-breaks during camp.
  • Job-relevant experience leading and managing tasks.
  • Assisting instructors, learning teaching and leadership skills.
